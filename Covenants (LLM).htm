What is a Covenant?

H3772 כָּרַת kârath, kaw-rath' — a primitive root; to cut (off, down or asunder); by implication, to destroy or consume; specifically, to covenant (i.e. make an alliance or bargain, originally by cutting flesh and passing between the pieces): be chewed, be con-(feder-)ate, covenant, cut (down, off), destroy, fail, feller, be freed, hew (down), make a league (covenant), × lose, perish, × utterly, × want.
G1242 διαθήκη diathḗkē, dee-ath-ay'-kay — from G1303; properly, a disposition, i.e. (specially) a contract (especially a devisory will): covenant, testament.

Think of a covenant as the making of a sacred agreement of which both parties swear that they will not break.

There are the promises that each party commits in the agreement. In this covenant I swear that I will xxx. These promises can also be called blessings.

Blessed be Jehovah who has given rest to His people Israel, according to all that He has spoken; not one word of all His good Word has fallen, which He spoke by the hand of His servant Moses. 1 Kings 8:56

There also are promises that each party makes that they will do if the other party breaks the covenant. If you break the covenant then I will hunt you down and kill you, and if I don't kill you then my children will hunt down you and your children to kill you. These are also called curses.

When the covenant is made it takes place in what is called a ceremony. In the ceremony there is a "thing" that confirms that each party swears that they will not break the covenant. It can be giving the other party something that is owned by you that is treasured. This "Xxx" is most important to me so I am giving it to you so you know that I am serious about making this covenant. These are called tokens of the covenant.

Is making a promise a sign that a covenant has been made?

And Jehovah God said to the serpent, Because you have done this, you are cursed above all beasts, and above every animal of the field. You shall go on your belly, and you shall eat dust all the days of your life. And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He will bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel. Genesis 3:14-15

Because God gave this promise to the Serpent does it mean that God has made a covenant with the Serpent?

Just because God promises something doesn't mean that He has made a covenant with them.

Phineas, the son of Eleazar, the son of Aaron took a spear and killed an Israelite man and a Moabite woman in the act of ritual prostitution inside the Tent of Meeting itself Numbers 25:6-8. Such was his zeal that the Lord made a covenant of peace with him that he and his descendants would enjoy an everlasting priesthood Numbers 25:10-13. The line of Phineas served as high priest down until the time of Eli and his sons (of the line of Ithamar according to 1 Chronicles 24:3) described in 1 Sam. 1-3. When the Tabernacle and priesthood was restored by David it was Zadok, a descendent of Phineas who served as high priest and so on down to the time of Ezra Ezra 7:1-5. The succession ended in 171 BC when Antiochus IV transferred the priesthood from Jason to Menelaus.

Ezra was a descendant of Phinehas:

And after these things, in the reign of Artaxerxes the king of Persia, Ezra the son of Seraiah... the son of Abishua, the son of Phinehas, the son of Eleazar, the son of Aaron the head priest. Ezra 7:1-5

These promises led to several developments in Hebrew theology.

a) The City of God. David's city, located on Mount Zion became the focus of soteriological and eschatological interest, rather than the whole of the Promised Land (Psalm 48:2; Isaiah 2:2-4; cf. Revelation 21). It also led to the belief that Jerusalem would never fall, a belief shattered by the conquest by Nebuchadnezzar of Babylon.

b) The Messiah. The idea of an ideal Davidite is developed in the Psalms and the prophetic writings. This "anointed one" or "Messiah" would rule the nations from Zion and serve as a priest after the order of Melchizedek (Psalm 2; Psalm 110). Like his forefather he would be born in Bethlehem in Judah (Micah 5:2; cf. Matthew 2:1-6).
